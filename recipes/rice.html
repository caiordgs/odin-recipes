White Rice

Description:

Plain white rice is an excellent inexpensive side dish for nearly any meal—it's hard to find a dish that rice doesn't complement. It's also a blank canvas to which you can add countless ingredients for extra flavor, and it does a good job of soaking up the sauces of whatever you pair it with, too. Once you discover how well this method works, you won't hesitate to cook rice for dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter, optional
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley, optional

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice until the water runs clear. Drain in a colander and set aside.
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring the 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the salt, stir, and then add the rinsed and drained rice. Stir with a fork.
  3. Reduce the heat, cover the rice, and let it simmer for 20 minutes. Check it after 15 minutes to see if all the water has evaporated. If it has, the rice is ready. If not, replace the lid and let the rice simmer an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from the heat, fluff with a fork, and transfer into a serving dish. You can also stir in 1 tablespoon of butter or add chopped parsley or other herbs to the pot before serving. Enjoy.
